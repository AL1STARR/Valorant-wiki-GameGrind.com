Introduction to VALORANT

Valorant is a free-to-play, team-based first-person tactical hero shooter developed and published by Riot Games. Set in a near-future Earth, the game pits two teams of five players against each other, with each player assuming the role of an "Agent"—characters inspired by various cultures and countries. Each Agent possesses unique abilities, adding strategic depth to the traditional gunplay.

The core gameplay revolves around objective-based modes, where one team attacks by planting a device called the "Spike," while the other defends. Valorant combines precise shooting mechanics reminiscent of Counter-Strike with the character-driven abilities found in games like Overwatch, creating a unique and competitive experience.​


HISTORY

Development of Valorant began in 2014 under the codename "Project A." Riot Games officially announced the game in March 2020, with a closed beta launching in April 2020. The game was officially released for Windows on June 2, 2020. In June 2024, Valorant expanded to consoles, launching on PlayStation 5 and Xbox Series X/S, though cross-play between PC and console is not supported.​

The development team aimed to create a tactical shooter that was both accessible to newcomers and deep enough for competitive play. By focusing on performance optimization and low system requirements, Riot ensured that Valorant could run smoothly on a wide range of hardware, broadening its potential player base.
